MOTION CAPTURE TECHNOLOGY

Motion capture technology or mocap is the process of recording the movements of an object or person and applying a 3D rig or animation to it. Mocap actors use a special suit referred to as the motion capture suit which uses sensors and markers to track the wearer's movements. The data will then be processed by a computer program to create a digital skeleton which is animated for a more realistic and accurate animation.

Mocap technology was first popularised through the use in films in 2002, and has since been used in various forms of media, mainly in films, gaming animations, online streaming, etc.
